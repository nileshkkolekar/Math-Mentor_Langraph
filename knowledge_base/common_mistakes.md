# Common mistakes and pitfalls

## Algebra
- Forgetting \( \sqrt{x^2} = |x| \), not \( x \).
- Dividing by an expression that could be zero without checking.
- Sign errors when expanding \( (a-b)^2 \) or moving terms across inequalities.

## Probability
- Confusing P(A|B) with P(B|A) (Bayes).
- Using addition rule without subtracting \( P(A \cap B) \) when events not mutually exclusive.
- Assuming independence when not stated.

## Calculus
- Forgetting chain rule in derivatives.
- Treating indeterminate forms as if they were numbers (e.g. 0/0 = 0).
- Missing endpoints in optimization on closed intervals.

## Units and domain
- Always state domain (e.g. x > 0) when it matters.
- Check that final answer satisfies original constraints.
